1. The Finding — Non-Monotonic ρ

The TBTF extension introduces an IRB bilateral exposure cap (eq. 6) that creates an emergent zombie bank channel not present in the base Lenzu-Tedeschi / Berardi-Tedeschi model.

Shock bankruptcies decrease monotonically with ρ (the professor's expected channel). But rationing and contagion deaths increase at moderate ρ, creating a non-monotonic total. This is not a bug — it is an emergent property of the bilateral exposure cap.

2. The Mechanism — Zombie Lending Channel

  1. Step 1 — Fire sale survival creates zombies. A bank facing a liquidity shortfall resorts to a fire sale. The accounting cost of recovering an amount a is a/ρ, deducted from total assets L. At ρ=0.1: cost = 10× amount → exceeds L → instant bankruptcy → fresh replacement enters. At ρ=0.4: cost = 2.5× amount → survivable → bank persists with reduced L, E. At ρ=0.9: cost = 1.11× amount → barely scratched.
  2. Step 2 — Zombies degrade lending via the IRB bilateral cap.
    Eq. 6: Lij = min( (γ·Ei + pj·(1−bj)·α·Aj) / (pj·(1−bj·η)) , Ci )
    A zombie's depleted equity Ei shrinks the term γ·Ei, collapsing the bilateral exposure cap. The professor's base model has no bilateral cap — lenders supply min(demand, Ci). Eq. 6 makes weak banks toxic to the network.
  3. Step 3 — Rationing cascade. When a zombie becomes a lender, its tiny bilateral cap rations borrowers. Rationed borrowers must fire-sell, potentially creating more zombies — a positive feedback loop.
  4. Step 4 — Delayed contagion. Zombies persist for multiple periods, accumulating interbank exposure. When they finally fail: ρ·L − D < 0 for all ρ < 1.125 (since D=135 > L=120) — the lender recovers nothing. Bad debt equals the full loan, driving lender equity negative: a contagion death.
Data proof: Rationing deaths: 5,700 (ρ=0) → 7,166 (ρ=0.8) = +1,466 extra deaths from the zombie lending channel. Contagion deaths: 457 (ρ=0) → 1,270 (ρ=0.4) = zombie deaths are more damaging than immediate failures.
Note on ρ=0.9: There are 13,736 fire sale survivors but only 248 total deaths and 0 contagion. Fire sales are so cheap that "zombies" heal naturally. The dangerous zone is moderate ρ (0.3–0.8) where banks are weak enough to eventually die but strong enough to persist.

3. The Role of η — Cushioning Zombie Deaths

η does not prevent zombie formation (that is purely a ρ phenomenon). η cushions zombie deaths: when a zombie finally fails, the bailout absorbs η·l, reducing bad debt to the lender. Contagion drops from 1,270 (η=0) to 719 (η=0.9).

When bailouts are free (no-tax regime), more η is always weakly beneficial — monotonically decreasing total bankruptcies. Under socialized tax, an interior optimum emerges at η*≈0.1 because the fiscal drag from the crisis tax eventually outweighs the bailout cushion.

4. Resolution Fund — The Lehman Surprise

Banks price loans using eq. 6 with the full policy η — they do not observe the fund balance. At η>0, the denominator (1−bj·η) shrinks, inflating loans. But the fund starts empty (fund_initial_balance=0) and recharges slowly (τ=0.0001).

At low η (fund can cover demand): the resolution fund outperforms free bailouts — fiscal discipline without depletion.

At high η (demand overwhelms fund): loans are inflated by η expectations, but bailouts fail when the fund runs dry. Moral hazard is priced in, the protection is gone. This is the Lehman mechanism: counterparties priced exposure assuming bailout, the guarantee evaporated.

Note on ρ=0.1 with resolution fund: Even η=0.1 is worse than η=0. The fund can never accumulate enough to cover even modest bailout demand, but loans are already distorted by η expectations.

5. Summary

  1. The IRB bilateral cap (eq. 6) introduces a zombie lending channel: fire sale survivors with depleted equity cannot lend effectively, creating rationing cascades. This is absent from the base model which has no bilateral exposure limits.
  2. The zombie channel creates non-monotonic ρ dynamics: moderate fire-sale recovery rates (0.3–0.8) are paradoxically more dangerous than extreme rates, because they allow insolvent banks to persist and degrade network lending capacity.
  3. The resolution fund creates a moral hazard trap: banks price loans assuming available bailouts, but the fund's finite capacity means the guarantee can evaporate — reproducing the Lehman Brothers surprise mechanism in a controlled simulation environment.
